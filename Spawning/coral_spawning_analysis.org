* Overview
This document summarizes coral spawning patterns across seven coral families included in Randall et al. (2024). The main idea behind this analysis is severalfold:

Generate accurate measures of when the species are spawning, so that I can create a particle tracking exercise that mimics the spawning times for most families and is statistically robust.

Generate probability distributions that can be used later to run bootstraps over the generated particle tracking outputs.

I’m using the data shared by Christopher Doropoulos, and all the analyses are contained in two R scripts: one to calculate the time window (in days) for the spawning event, and a second R script to calculate the best time (hour) of day when corals start and stop spawning.

Dataset source: Baird et al. (2021) Indo-Pacific coral spawning database.
Total observations analyzed: 1268
Families: 7
Genera: 28
Species: 131

* Global Analysis

** Spawning by Hour of Day

Analysis of spawning time across all families combined.

[[file:global_spawning_hour.png]]

*Key finding:* Peak spawning occurs at hour 21 (9:00 PM), with  30.0%  of all spawning events. Most spawning (>95%) occurs between 19:00-23:00.

** Spawning by Days After Full Moon

Analysis of spawning timing relative to lunar cycle.

[[file:global_spawning_moon.png]]

*Key finding:* Peak spawning occurs  4  nights after full moon, with  27.1%  of events. Most spawning (>90%) occurs 2-7 nights after full moon.

* Family-Level Analysis

** Spawning by Hour of Day (Per Family)

Spawning time patterns vary slightly among families but show consistent evening timing.

[[file:family_spawning_hour.png]]

*Key finding:* All families spawn predominantly between 20:00-22:00, with median times ranging from  20.0  to  22.0  hours.

** Spawning by Days After Full Moon (Per Family)

Lunar timing shows family-specific patterns with overlapping windows.

[[file:family_spawning_moon.png]]

*Key finding:* Median spawning ranges from  4  to  6  nights after full moon. Acroporidae and Poritidae spawn earlier (median 4 nights), while Lobophylliidae and Diploastreidae spawn later (median 6 nights).

* Key Conclusions

1. *Temporal synchronization:* Spawning is highly synchronized across families, occurring predominantly 20:00-22:00.
2. *Lunar synchronization:* Most spawning occurs 3-6 nights after full moon, with family-specific variation.
3. *Mass spawning:* Tight temporal clustering facilitates cross-fertilization and mass spawning events.
4. *Family differences:* While timing is similar, Acroporidae shows earlier lunar timing (median 4 nights) compared to Lobophylliidae (median 6 nights).
