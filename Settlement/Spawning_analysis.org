* Executive Summary

This document presents a comprehensive analysis of coral spawning patterns for the families included in Randall et al. (2024) "Larval precompetency and settlement behaviour in 25 Indo-Pacific coral species". The analysis is based on the Baird et al. (2021) coral spawning database and focuses on the seven families that have both spawning and settlement data available.

** Key Findings Summary

*** Data Coverage
- 1,268 spawning observations across 7 families
- 28 genera, 131 species
- Acroporidae: 830 observations (65% of dataset)
- Merulinidae: 316 observations (25% of dataset)

*** Spawning Timing Patterns
- **Season**: Peak spawning October-December (austral spring)
- **Lunar Cycle**: 3-6 nights after full moon (median 4-5 nights)
- **Time of Day**: Evening hours 20:00-21:00 (8-9 PM)
- **Duration**: Start ~21:00h, end ~21:00-22:00h

*** Family-Specific Patterns
All families show remarkable consistency in:
1. Spawning season (Oct-Dec)
2. Lunar timing (3-6 nights post-full moon)
3. Time of day (evening, 20-21h)

This synchronization facilitates mass spawning events critical for reef connectivity.

The following families from Randall et al. (2024) are analyzed:

1. **Acroporidae** - Fast-growing branching corals (Acropora, Montipora, Astreopora)
2. **Merulinidae** - Massive/brain corals (Goniastrea, Favites, Dipsastraea, etc.)
3. **Poritidae** - Massive/branching forms (Porites, Goniopora)
4. **Diploastreidae** - Diploastrea species
5. **Agariciidae** - Plate corals (Agaricia, Pavona, Leptoseris)
6. **Lobophylliidae** - Brain/massive corals (Lobophyllia, Symphyllia, etc.)
7. **Euphylliidae** - Bubble corals (Euphyllia, Catalaphyllia, etc.)

** Analysis Approach
1. Filter spawning database to include only Randall families
2. Analyze spawning seasonality by family
3. Examine spawning time of day patterns
4. Compare with settlement timing from Randall et al. (2024)
5. Identify potential mismatches between spawning and settlement timing

* Key Findings

** Spawning Seasonality
- Most families show peak spawning in November-December (Southern Hemisphere spring)
- Some variation in spawning windows between families
- Acroporidae and Merulinidae show the most spawning observations

** Spawning Time of Day
- Most spawning occurs in evening hours (18:00-24:00)
- **Acroporidae**: Median start 21:00h (mean 20.7h ± 1.5 SD)
- **Merulinidae**: Median start 21:00h (mean 20.8h ± 1.1 SD)
- **Poritidae**: Median start 20.9h (mean 20.9h ± 1.1 SD)
- Families show similar evening spawning patterns

** Full Moon Timing
- Spawning occurs 0-12 nights after full moon
- **Acroporidae**: Median 4 nights after full moon (mean 3.9 ± 1.9 SD, n=830)
- **Merulinidae**: Median 5 nights after full moon (mean 4.8 ± 1.1 SD, n=316)
- **Poritidae**: Median 4 nights after full moon (mean 4.0 ± 1.1 SD, n=72)
- **Lobophylliidae**: Median 6 nights after full moon (mean 5.4 ± 1.5 SD, n=43)
- Most families spawn 3-6 nights after full moon

** Settlement Timing (from Randall et al. 2024)
- **Acroporidae**: First settlement day 6, peak day 41, up to 76 days
- **Merulinidae**: First settlement day 6, peak day 59, up to 77 days  
- **Euphylliidae**: First settlement day 5, peak day 5, up to 36 days
- **Poritidae**: First settlement day 4, peak day 4, up to 31 days
- **Diploastreidae**: First settlement day 38, peak day 38, up to 38 days

* Ecological Implications

** Spawning-Settlement Timing Mismatch
The analysis reveals potential mismatches between spawning timing and settlement competence:

1. **Early Spawners, Late Settlers**: Some families spawn early in the season but have long precompetency periods
2. **Late Spawners, Early Settlers**: Some families spawn late but settle quickly
3. **Extended Settlement Windows**: Most families show very long settlement periods (30-77 days)

** Connectivity Implications
- Long settlement windows may increase connectivity potential
- Family-specific spawning and settlement timing affects larval dispersal
- Seasonal spawning patterns may influence connectivity across different times of year

* Data Quality and Limitations

** Spawning Data
- Global dataset with varying data quality
- Some families have limited observations
- Spawning times may be location-specific

** Settlement Data
- Based on laboratory experiments
- Control conditions only (no settlement cues)
- May not reflect natural settlement patterns
- Low overall settlement rates (0.3-3.5%)

* Recommendations

** For Connectivity Modeling
1. Use family-specific spawning and settlement parameters
2. Account for long settlement windows in dispersal models
3. Consider seasonal variation in spawning timing
4. Validate models with field settlement data

** For Future Research
1. Collect more settlement data for underrepresented families
2. Investigate settlement cues and natural settlement rates
3. Study spawning-settlement timing relationships in the field
4. Develop family-specific competence functions
